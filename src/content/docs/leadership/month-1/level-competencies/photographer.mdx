# Photographer Competencies

<div class="gradient-panel">
  <p class="text-[17px] leading-7 text-[#1a1f36]">
    These competencies outline the expectations and growth path for photographers at each level. Use this framework to understand your current level and what's needed to advance to the next level.
  </p>
</div>

## Junior Photographer (P1)

<div class="competencies-table">
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th style="width: 150px;">Area</th>
<th>Competencies</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Skill (Individual)</td>
<td>• Developing understanding of core photography skills and beginning to apply these skills to small or medium-sized projects<br />• Assisting Senior Photographer in studio and on set<br />• Beginning to create photos that reflect our brand and clearly communicate our story</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Influence (Team)</td>
<td>• Developing your ability to present visual design decisions clearly<br />• Beginning to influence decision-making for brand design projects among your core team</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Thinking (Team)</td>
<td>• Spending time fully understanding project needs and partnering with your manager to contribute to the coordination and organization of assigned projects<br />• Starting to propose improvements to existing processes<br />• Contributing to project planning and coordination<br />• Learning to create clear timelines and ownership responsibilities and communicating clearly with project partners</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>

## Photographer (P2)

<div class="competencies-table">
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th style="width: 150px;">Area</th>
<th>Competencies</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Skill (Individual)</td>
<td>• Solid understanding of core photography skills and confidently applying these skills to small to medium-sized brand photo projects<br />• Utilizing our existing photography system to create compelling images with strong composition and aesthetics<br />• Creating designs that reflect our brand and clearly communicate our story</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Influence (Team)</td>
<td>• Clearly and effectively presenting your visual design decisions<br />• Beginning to build compelling arguments that consider brand design, as well as business and organizational needs<br />• Influencing decisions among your core team and beginning to drive broader stakeholder alignment</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Thinking (Team)</td>
<td>• Spending time fully understanding project needs and contributing to project planning processes<br />• Proposing improvements to existing processes, prioritizing your work, and tracking project goals and progress<br />• Leading steps in the project planning process, such as ownership assignments, crafting budgets, and tracking deliverables</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>

## Senior Photographer (P3)

<div class="competencies-table">
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th style="width: 150px;">Area</th>
<th>Competencies</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Skill (Individual)</td>
<td>• Strong understanding of core photography skills and confidently applying these skills to large projects<br />• Consistently creating visuals that reflect our brand and clearly communicate our story<br />• Beginning to concept new visual directions and overarching narratives for projects</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Influence (Team)</td>
<td>• Proactively developing the approach, design stages, processes, and timeline to deliver on brand design projects<br />• Effectively presenting visual design decisions and building compelling arguments<br />• Proactively influencing decisions and working with leaders from other teams to drive broader alignment</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Thinking (Team)</td>
<td>• Spending time fully understanding organizational needs and establishing clear processes with multiple stakeholders or teams<br />• Prioritizing work effectively based on business and customer needs and documenting your thinking with minimal guidance<br />• Comfortable working with project tools to document your work and defining new tools, templates, or systems to recommend better ways of working</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>

</tr>
